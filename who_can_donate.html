Who can donate?

Who can give blood

  • Donor(s) should be generally fit and well.
  • Donor(s) should be aged between 17 and 65.
  • Donor(s) should be weigh between 7 stone 12 lbs (50kg) and 25 stone (158kg).
  • Donor(s) should be have suitable veins (we will check these before you donate).
  • Donor(s) should be meet all donor eligibility criteria (we will check this with you before you donate)

Who can't give blood

  • Donor(s) who have had most types of cancer.
  • Donor(s) who have some heart conditions
  • Donor(s) who have received blood, platelets, plasma or any other blood products after 1 January 1980
  • Donor(s) who have tested positive for HIV.
  • Donor(s) who have had an organ transplant.
  • Donor(s) who are a hepatitis B carrier.
  • Donor(s) who are a hepatitis C carrier
  • Donor(s) who have injected non-prescribed drugs including body-building and injectable tanning agents. You may be able to give if a doctor prescribed the drugs
